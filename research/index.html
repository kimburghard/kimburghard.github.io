Papers & Articles

Circadian Rhythm Disruption in Gastrointestinal Disorders - 2023

Abstract: This paper reviews several studies concerning the link between gastrointestinal disorders and circadian clock dysregulation. Animal and human trials indicate that certain GI conditions such as IBD are strongly associated with disrupted circadian rhythms. I provide an overview of several studies on the topics of sleep quality, clinical fatigue, and chronic inflammation, and propose inflammatory response dysregulation and impaired apoptosis-regeneration homeostasis as underlying mechanisms.



A Review of the Relationship Between ADHD and Emotional Dysregulation - 2023

Abstract: With ADHD prevalence in American children reaching up to 12.9% (2023), understanding its mechanisms is critical for improving diagnosis and treatment. This review examines emotional dysregulation as a key component of ADHD functional impairment. I investigate the role of dopamine receptor perturbation and abnormal rates of reward responsiveness in the ventral striatum of ADHD individuals as a primary mechanism of emotional dysregulation. I also connect these mechanisms to related features of ADHD, such as rejection-sensitive dysphoria, low frustration tolerance, and high rates of comorbidity with depression.



Dissociation and Stress Disorders: A Review and Research Proposal - 2023

Abstract: Many individuals who endure some form of dissociative or stress disorder, including PTSD and DPDRD, experience an often-overlooked class of symptoms called depersonalization/derealization (DEP/DER). These symptoms concern feelings of disconnectedness from the sense of self, the body, and the physical environment, with underlying causes related to integrative failure of fundamental mental functions. This proposal addresses a gap in the clinical research for DEP/DER, namely, the lack of studies using experimental evidence in favor of self-report measures for experiences of DEP/DER. I recommend an increased emphasis on the neuroanalytic perspective to gain a well-rounded view of these symptoms utilizing functional neuroimaging techniques. I also highlight the role of prefrontal cortex hyperactivation, multisensory integration deficits, and structural changes in attentional networks in these conditions as particular points of interest.
